Reducing Electronic Waste for a Cleaner Future

Teaching communities how to responsibly recycle old tech, extend device lifespans, and protect our environment from toxic waste.

See What We Have To Offer

E-waste is growing at an alarming rate because many people don't know how smartphones affect our planet. Old phones are thrown into the trash, releasing harmful materials into the environment instead of being recycled safely.

Smartphones are packed with precious, high-grade metals like gold, silver, and palladium. Instead of letting these finite resources go to waste in landfills like Htein Pin, our service ensures 100% of these rare materials are safely extracted and fed back into Myanmar's local tech-refurbishing cycle.

So many outdated mobile phones sit forgotten in drawers across Yangon households because people don't know how to dispose of them. Our service provides a convenient collection point so these idle devices don't end up mixed with regular household trash collected by the YCDC.

Up to 80% of a smartphone's lifetime carbon footprint happens during factory production and raw mineral mining. By recycling your old device with us, you help reduce the global demand for new mining—directly easing the environmental strain on mineral-rich areas near our borders.

When phones are thrown into standard trash bins, toxic heavy metals like lead and mercury leak into the surrounding soil and water tables. Our professional recycling service completely eliminates open-burning risks and landfill pollution, actively protecting the urban environment and community health across Yangon.

The Cost of Burning Tech

When smartphones end up in landfills or open burn pits, they don't just sit there—they poison our surroundings.

Smartphones contain toxic heavy metals like lead, mercury, and cadmium. When buried or burned, these hazardous materials leak into local soil and water systems, harming agriculture and wildlife.

Burning electronic components releases highly dangerous chemicals into the air. Breathing these toxic fumes causes severe respiratory illnesses and long-term health damage for communities living nearby.

The Power of Manual Dismantling

Shifting from raw destruction to careful, hand-separated e-waste management protects both our resources and our planet.

Unlike large industrial shredders that smash devices and mix toxic chemicals blindly, manual dismantling isolates hazardous parts like batteries and mercury switches before any crushing happens. This keeps the surrounding soil and water completely clean.

Technicians can visually identify and cleanly remove pure components like copper wiring, gold-plated connectors, and silver solders. This precision keeps the recovered metals highly pure and instantly ready for reuse in new technology.

By dismantling devices carefully on specialized workbenches with proper ventilation, we completely eliminate the need to burn e-waste. This keeps dangerous heavy metal dust and toxic chemical fumes out of the air our communities breathe.

How It Works?
Step By Step

1.

Mechanical Shredding

Stripped smartphone chassis are fed into heavy industrial shredders that use high-torque rotating blades to crush the tech into uniform, coin-sized pieces.

2.

Magnetic Separation

The shredded mixed streams pass under powerful overhead magnets and eddy-current separators to instantly isolate iron, steel, and aluminum from plastics.

3.

Advanced Recovery

The remaining circuit board fragments undergo automated air classification and optical sorting to group high-value gold and copper particles together.

The Power of Shredding & Recovery

When smartphones end up in landfills or open burn pits, they don't just sit there—they poison our surroundings.

Manual dismantling is great for removing dangerous parts, but it is too slow for millions of tons of waste. Large-scale industrial shredders can process thousands of devices every hour, making global tech recycling fast and highly efficient.

The platform uses advanced sorting tech like "air classifiers" that blow air through the shredded mix. Heavy metals drop straight down, while lighter materials like plastics fly into separate bins automatically.

Once sorted into pure piles of plastic, copper, aluminum, and gold dust, these materials are sent to manufacturing plants. They are melted down and used to build brand-new eco-friendly electronics without mining new resources.
