The Environmental
Impact of Smartphones

From rare-earth mineral mining to energy-intensive data centers, our constant connectivity comes with a significant environmental price tag. Explore how your device affects the planet and what you can do to minimize your carbon footprint.

Resource Extraction

Mining for cobalt and lithium destroys ecosystems and consumes massive amounts of water.

E-Waste Crisis

Millions of tons of smartphones are discarded yearly, leaking toxic chemicals into the soil.

Carbon Footprint

Manufacturing a single phone generates about 80% of its total lifetime CO2 emissions.

Energy Usage

Charging billions of devices and powering the cloud requires vast amounts of electricity.

The Problem with E-Waste

Every year, millions of tons of electronic devices are discarded, creating a global environmental crisis. These items contain toxic materials like lead and mercury that can leak into soil and water, but they also contain valuable metals that are lost when not recycled properly.

Sustainable
Solutions

Modern recycling facilities use specialized machinery and manual labor to safely dismantle electronics. This ensures that hazardous materials are contained while valuable components are recovered for future use.

01
Collection & Sorting
Devices are gathered and categorized by type. Sorting ensures that batteries and hazardous components are handled separately.
02
Manual Dismantling
Skilled technicians remove specific parts like circuit boards, screens, and processors. This step preserves the quality of high-value materials like gold and copper.
03
Shredding & Recovery
Remaining materials are shredded and passed through magnets and water systems to separate plastics from metals, which are then melted down to create new products.
