Smartphones have one of the highest replacement rates in modern consumer tech. Millions are discarded yearly, and because they contain concentrated rare-earth metals alongside hazardous toxins like lead and mercury, they are both highly valuable to recover and highly dangerous if left in a landfill.

Manual dismantling is the safe pre-processing phase where technicians remove batteries and sensitive glass screens by hand. Industrial shredding takes the remaining shell and crushes it mechanically at a massive scale to separate pure streams of metals and high-grade plastics.

Extracting precious metals through circular recycling pipelines uses a fraction of the energy required for traditional raw mining operations. Safely recovering copper, gold, and aluminum from existing e-waste cuts carbon emissions by up to 99% compared to pulling new materials from the earth.
